Frequently Asked Questions

What is Comprehensive Coverage?

The Comprehensive coverage is also known as the “full coverage” it covers your vehicle from damages that occur not just from collisions but also from hail, wind, flood, vandalism, theft etc.

What is General Exclusions?

The term general exclusions refer to all those expenses which are not covered in your car insurance policy.

Day to day mechanical repairs for instance engine servicing, break-pad maintenance etc.

What is the difference between Sum Insured and Market Value?

Market value refers to the evaluation of the car in the market, Sum insured it is the value of your car which you have provided to your insurer for coverage.

In 2020 a person declared a car value for 20 lacs which is the sum insured however the market value may not be the same over a certain period of time and may increase and decrease. the market value can change to 23 lacs or 19 lacs.

What is Total Loss?

Total loss is a condition whereby the insurance company declares the car to be beyond repairs. The insurer gives a wholesome amount to the insured rather than repairing its car. In this case, the insured will get lower of the market value or sum insured.

If a car is declared a total loss and if the sum insured of the car was 25 lacs and the market value was 27 lacs, in this case, the assured will get 25 lacs because it is lower of both amounts.

What is Depreciation?

Depreciation refers to the wear and tear of the car, as your car gets older it is the percentage of the claim which the insurer does not pay for in case of an accident. Depreciation is only applied to car parts.

Let’s assume you get into an accident and the dealership recommends the repair cost is 200,000 which comprises 100,000 as labour expenses and 100,000 for car parts if the depreciation is 50% in a specific year. You will get full compensation for labour and 50% depreciation on parts leading to the payout on parts to be 50,00 making the full payout to be 150,000.
